  Slide 1 — Problem Statement

  Headline: Mental Healthcare is Broken for the People Who Need It Most

  Bullets:

  - Accessibility Gap: 85% of people with mental health disorders in low-to-middle income countries receive NO treatment (WHO, 2022). Geographic and financial    
  barriers block access to qualified therapists.
  - Stigma Barrier: 40% of those who recognize they need help avoid seeking it due to fear of judgment. Traditional clinic-based therapy is visibly public and    
  socially costly.
  - Privacy Crisis: Patients fear sharing sensitive mental health data. Existing digital health apps store unencrypted records on centralized servers — a single  
  data breach exposes lifetime mental health history.
  - Therapist Scarcity: India has fewer than 1 mental health professional per 100,000 people (NIMHANS). The demand-supply gap cannot be filled by human
  professionals alone.
  - Fragmented Care: Users switch between multiple apps for mood tracking, therapist booking, journaling, and wellness content. No single platform connects all   
  these touchpoints.

  One-liner: NeuroNet 2.0 exists because mental healthcare should be accessible, private, and personalized — not gatekept behind waiting rooms, costs, and stigma.

  Slide 4 — Go-to-Market Strategy

  Target Segments (Prioritized):

  1. Primary: College students (18–25) — highest mental health burden, highest tech adoption
  2. Secondary: Young professionals (25–35) — stress, burnout, work-life imbalance
  3. Tertiary: Corporate HR departments — employee wellness programs
  4. Niche: Rural India (Marathi/Hindi speakers) — underserved, high stigma, offline-first value

  GTM Phases:

  Phase 1 — Community-Led (Month 1–4)
  - Launch in 5 engineering/medical college campuses (Pune, Mumbai, Hyderabad)
  - Partner with NSS cells, student mental health clubs, Vandrevala Foundation
  - Student ambassador program: early access + referral credits
  - Target: 1,000 active users

  Phase 2 — Creator & Influencer (Month 4–8)
  - Mental health YouTubers, Instagram therapists (100K–500K followers)
  - NeuroPet "meet your pet" viral challenge campaign
  - Hindi/Marathi content creators to reach Tier 2/3 cities
  - Podcast partnerships with mental health shows
  - Target: 10,000 users

  Phase 3 — B2B & Institutional (Month 8–18)
  - Corporate wellness packages (₹50–200/employee/month)
  - Hospital/clinic white-labeling: license NeuroNet AI for outpatient support
  - Insurance partnerships: mental health check-ins as policy rider
  - Government NIMHANS / NITI Aayog pilot programs
  - Target: 1 Lakh users

  Therapist Acquisition Strategy:
  - Onboard 50 verified therapists in Phase 1 via direct outreach + iCall/AASRA networks
  - Revenue share: therapist keeps 85% of session fee
  - NeuroNet takes 15% platform commission
  - Verified badge + profile visibility drives organic acquisition

  Retention Mechanics:
  - NeuroPet "relationship" builds over time (memory, personality)
  - AVC progress streaks and certifications
  - Weekly AI-generated mood report emails
  - Community group belonging (Firebase Firestore groups)
